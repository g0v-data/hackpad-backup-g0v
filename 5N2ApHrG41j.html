TEDxTaipei 2013 Intro

Kirby Wu - One of g0v.tw’s founding members.  A software engineer and web developer who loves hiking and photography.

In 0ct., 2012, the 45-second "Ecnomocis Boosting" advertisement for promoting Taiwan government policy was broadcasted publicly. While it costs 4.5 million NT dollars, it conveyed an obfuscating message that it’s not citizens’ business to understand what the government is doing.  As a result of frustration in the government, the first g0v hackathon was held by in 2012.12.1. More people than expected turned up in the hackathon, finally led to the emerging of the community.

g0v.tw devotes both in promoting open data and developing software platforms for civic participation in public affair. By replacing the "o" in gov with "0", g0v represents both contemplation about the role of government and perspective of the digital generation. Based on open source concept, g0v involves in freedom of both speech and information by coding to provide citizens information services which are easy to use.

Information transparency can help citizens understand how government works and public issues better, which will not be monopolized by mass media. Thus the government can be well-supervised, and then we can consolidate the foundation of republic in our country.
