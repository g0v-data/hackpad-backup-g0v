Press Notice: Taiwanese to organize world wide assembly supporting same-sex marriage on Human Rights Day

Taiwan, a democratic country who elect its own president and congress, is currently under intensive debate on whether to recognize same-sex marriage. The legislative process, i.e. the amendment of the Taiwanese Civil Code, which acquired the support of the majority of citizens according to a poll done in 2013, is now under strong pressure from not only pro-LGBT activists, but also conservative religeous groups. On Decenber third, some 100,000 people rallied on Ketagalan Blvd. in Taipei, Taiwan, protesting against the legislation. In response, pro-LGBT groups in Taiwan is organizing an assembly to support the legislation on December 10th, the Human Rights Day, on Ketagalan Blvd., in the belief that the pursuit of equal rights to marry for homosexual align with the core values of the Human Rights Day. 

The assembly on Human Rights Day in Taiwan will be echoed world-wide by Taiwanese people in the same weekend in 28 different cities in the US, Canada, Germany, the Netherlands, UK, Switzerland, Norway, Japan, Australia, and Thailand. These assemblies were initiated by a press conference in Washington D.C. on 11/30, and since then obtained world-wide support from Taiwanese organizers with the help of Overseas Taiwanese for Democracy, an Taiwanese activist group.
